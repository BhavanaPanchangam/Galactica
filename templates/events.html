Historical Asteroid Events
A timeline of significant asteroid impacts and events
~65 Million Years Ago 10,000 BC 1490 1492 1803 1908 1947 2008 2013 2019 2020 2021

Chicxulub Impact

~65 Million Years Ago
Location: Yucatan Peninsula, Mexico
Details: The Chicxulub asteroid impact is believed to have caused the mass extinction of the dinosaurs. It created a crater over 150 km in diameter.

Campo del Cielo

~10,000 BC
Location: Argentina
Details: An iron meteorite impact created a large field of craters spread over 60 square kilometers, with some craters as deep as 5 meters.

Ch’ing-yang Event

1490
Location: Shaanxi Province, China
Details: Historical accounts suggest a meteor shower led to thousands of casualties, possibly due to meteor fragments hitting the ground.

Ensisheim Meteorite

November 7, 1492
Location: Ensisheim, Alsace (present-day France)
Details: The oldest recorded meteorite in Europe struck a wheat field, weighing 127 kilograms. It was preserved as a historical relic.

L’Aigle Meteorite Shower

April 26, 1803
Location: L’Aigle, France
Details: Over 3,000 fragments fell from the sky, helping to establish the extraterrestrial origin of meteorites in scientific studies.

Tunguska Event

June 30, 1908
Location: Tunguska, Siberia, Russia
Details: A massive explosion caused by an asteroid or comet flattened 2,000 square kilometers of forest, releasing energy equivalent to a large nuclear bomb.

Sikhote-Alin Meteorite

February 12, 1947
Location: Sikhote-Alin Mountains, Russia
Details: A large iron meteor exploded over the mountains, leaving behind numerous fragments and creating craters.

TC3 Impact

October 7, 2008
Location: Nubian Desert, Sudan
Details: A small asteroid (4 meters in diameter) was detected before entering Earth’s atmosphere and exploded mid-air, with fragments recovered later.

Chelyabinsk Meteor

February 15, 2013
Location: Chelyabinsk, Russia
Details: The meteor exploded mid-air, releasing energy 20–30 times more powerful than the Hiroshima bomb. Over 1,000 people were injured by the shockwave.

Near Miss by 2019 OK

July 25, 2019
Location: Near-Earth Orbit
Details: Asteroid 2019 OK, measuring 100 meters wide, passed within 73,000 kilometers of Earth—closer than the Moon.

Near Miss by 2020 QG

August 16, 2020
Location: Near-Earth Orbit
Details: Asteroid 2020 QG passed just 2,950 kilometers above Earth’s surface, marking one of the closest recorded asteroid flybys.

Near Miss by 2021 GW4

April 12, 2021
Location: Near-Earth Orbit
Details: Asteroid 2021 GW4 passed within 19,000 kilometers of Earth, making it one of the closest recorded encounters with a small asteroid.
