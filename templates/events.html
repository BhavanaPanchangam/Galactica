Historical Asteroid Events
A timeline of significant asteroid impacts and events

Chicxulub Impact

~65 Million Years Ago
Believed to have caused the extinction of the dinosaurs, this asteroid created a crater over 150 kilometers in diameter in present-day Mexico's Yucatan Peninsula.

Ch’ing-yang Event

1490
Historical records from China suggest that a meteor shower in the Shaanxi province led to thousands of casualties, possibly due to meteor fragments.

Tunguska Event

June 30, 1908
A massive explosion in Siberia flattened trees over 2,000 square kilometers. Scientists believe this was caused by an asteroid or comet airburst.

Sikhote-Alin Meteorite

February 12, 1947
A large iron meteor exploded over the Sikhote-Alin Mountains in Russia, leaving behind fragments that formed craters in the area.

Chelyabinsk Meteor

February 15, 2013
A meteor exploded over Chelyabinsk, Russia, releasing 20–30 times more energy than the Hiroshima atomic bomb, injuring over 1,000 people.

Near Miss by 2019 OK

July 25, 2019
The asteroid 2019 OK, measuring 100 meters wide, passed within 73,000 kilometers of Earth—closer than the Moon.

Near Miss by 2020 QG

August 16, 2020
The asteroid 2020 QG passed just 2,950 kilometers above Earth's surface, marking one of the closest approaches ever recorded.
